# Types of Audits

## Internal Audit

Performed by members of the organization to evaluate internal controls, operational effectiveness, and compliance before engaging external auditors.

### Purpose

- Improve internal processes
- Detect weaknesses early
- Reduce future audit costs
- Prepare for external assessments

---

## External Audit

Performed by independent auditing firms to provide objective assurance regarding compliance, governance, and financial or regulatory obligations.

### Purpose

- Independent verification
- Regulatory compliance
- Stakeholder confidence
- Certification readiness

---

## Third-Party Audit

Evaluates vendors, suppliers, contractors, or managed service providers to ensure they meet required security and compliance standards.

### Purpose

- Supply chain assurance
- Vendor risk management
- Contract compliance
- Data protection validation

# Audit Frameworks Studied

## COBIT

Focused on IT governance and management by aligning technology with business objectives through structured control objectives.

**Primary Areas**

- Governance
- Risk Management
- Resource Management
- Performance Monitoring

---

## ISO/IEC 27001

Provides a structured framework for establishing and maintaining an Information Security Management System (ISMS).

**Focus Areas**

- Information Security Policies
- Risk Management
- Asset Protection
- Continuous Improvement

---

## COSO

Designed to strengthen enterprise internal controls and improve organizational governance.

**Core Objectives**

- Internal Controls
- Fraud Prevention
- Risk Management
- Operational Effectiveness

---

## ITIL

Best-practice framework for IT Service Management (ITSM).

**Focus Areas**

- Incident Management
- Change Management
- Service Delivery
- Continuous Service Improvement

---

## PCI DSS

Security standard for organizations handling payment card information.

**Primary Controls**

- Secure Networks
- Access Control
- Encryption
- Vulnerability Management
- Continuous Monitoring

---

## ISAE 3402

Provides assurance regarding internal controls over financial reporting for service organizations.

---

## SOX

Regulatory framework requiring strong internal controls and accurate financial reporting for publicly traded companies.

# Audit Lifecycle

The audit followed a structured methodology consisting of five major phases.

## 1. Planning

Activities performed:

- Defined audit scope
- Established audit objectives
- Identified systems under review
- Determined applicable regulations
- Selected audit framework
- Developed audit plan

---

## 2. Execution

Performed evidence collection through:

- Documentation review
- Policy evaluation
- Interviews
- Infrastructure inspection
- Configuration review
- Security control verification

---

## 3. Assessment

During assessment I evaluated:

- Compliance gaps
- Security weaknesses
- Governance maturity
- Control effectiveness
- Risk exposure

Recommendations were created based on observed findings.

---

## 4. Reporting

Prepared an audit report containing:

- Executive findings
- Identified risks
- Control deficiencies
- Compliance observations
- Recommended remediation
- Overall audit conclusion

---

## 5. Follow-Up

Verified that corrective actions were implemented successfully by:

- Reviewing remediation evidence
- Confirming policy updates
- Reassessing identified risks
- Validating implemented controls

# Risk Assessment

The audit identified several categories of organizational risk requiring continuous governance.

| Risk Area | Potential Impact |
|------------|------------------|
| Weak Internal Controls | Increased operational risk |
| Poor Governance | Misalignment with business objectives |
| Non-Compliance | Regulatory penalties |
| Inadequate Monitoring | Delayed incident detection |
| Weak Access Controls | Unauthorized access |
| Insufficient Documentation | Audit failures |
| Ineffective Disaster Recovery | Extended downtime |

# Key Takeaways

This project reinforced several important concepts:

- Auditing extends beyond financial reviews into governance, security, and operational effectiveness.
- Effective audits rely on structured planning, objective evidence collection, and standardized frameworks.
- Governance frameworks such as COBIT and ISO/IEC 27001 help organizations align IT with business and security objectives.
- Continuous improvement depends on regular audits, remediation tracking, and follow-up assessments.
- Information systems auditing strengthens confidentiality, integrity, availability, and regulatory compliance.
