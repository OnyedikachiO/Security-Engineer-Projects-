# Project Overview

This project demonstrates the role of a **Cyber Crisis Management Team (CMT)** during high-severity cyber incidents. Rather than focusing on the technical investigation alone, the project explores how executive leadership, legal advisors, communications teams, and technical subject matter experts coordinate strategic decisions to protect business operations during a cyber crisis.

The project covers cyber crisis severity classification, Crisis Management Team roles, the Golden Hour response, crisis decision-making, stakeholder communications, regulatory obligations, and the importance of Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) in guiding executive decisions.

Throughout this project, I applied industry best practices aligned with incident response frameworks and cyber crisis management principles to understand how organizations respond when a security incident escalates into a business-wide crisis.

# Scenario

A large-scale cybersecurity incident escalated beyond the capabilities of the Security Operations Center (SOC) and Computer Security Incident Response Team (CSIRT).

Due to the business impact, the incident required activation of the **Crisis Management Team (CMT)**, responsible for making executive decisions that balanced security, business continuity, legal obligations, regulatory compliance, and organizational reputation.

Rather than performing technical remediation, the CMT coordinated strategic actions while relying on Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) to provide technical guidance.

---

# Phase 1 — Understanding a Cyber Crisis

Not every security incident becomes a cyber crisis.

Organizations classify incidents based on their impact and scope to determine the appropriate response level.

## Incident Severity Levels

| Level | Response Team | Example |
|---------|--------------|---------|
| Level 1 | SOC | Single phishing email reported |
| Level 2 | CERT | One compromised user account |
| Level 3 | CSIRT | Malware affecting multiple users |
| Level 4 | Crisis Management Team (CMT) | Enterprise-wide ransomware attack |

A Level 4 incident requires executive involvement because business-critical decisions must be made quickly.

### Key Learning

Cyber crises are defined not only by technical severity but also by their impact on business operations, customers, regulatory obligations, and organizational reputation.

---

# Phase 2 — Crisis Management Team (CMT)

A Crisis Management Team consists of senior leadership and key stakeholders responsible for directing the organization's response during major cyber incidents.

Unlike technical response teams, the CMT focuses on business decisions rather than technical implementation.

## CMT Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| Chair | Leads the crisis response and approves major decisions |
| Executives | Provide strategic business oversight |
| Communication | Manages internal and external messaging |
| Legal | Ensures compliance with laws and regulations |
| Operations | Maintains business continuity |
| Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) | Provide technical guidance |
| Scribe | Records all discussions and decisions |

### Key Learning

Effective cyber crisis management requires collaboration between technical experts and executive leadership.

Technical teams explain **what is happening**, while executives determine **what the organization should do**.

---

# Phase 3 — The Golden Hour

The first hour following activation of the Crisis Management Team is known as the **Golden Hour**.

Rapid and coordinated action during this period significantly improves an organization's ability to control the crisis.

## Golden Hour Activities

1. Assemble the Crisis Management Team
2. Receive a CSIRT briefing
3. Assess the scope and business impact
4. Prioritize immediate actions
5. Prepare stakeholder communications

### CSIRT Briefing Includes

- Current incident summary
- Actions already performed
- Immediate risks
- Recommended executive actions

### Key Learning

The Golden Hour establishes situational awareness and enables executives to make informed decisions before the crisis escalates further.

---

# Phase 4 — Crisis Management Process

Once the Golden Hour is complete, the Crisis Management Team enters a continuous decision-making cycle until the crisis is resolved.

## Continuous CMT Workflow

```text
Information Updates
        ↓
      Triage
        ↓
Action Discussions
        ↓
Action Approvals
        ↓
Implementation
        ↓
Repeat
```

During each cycle, Subject Matter Experts provide updated technical information while executives evaluate business impacts before approving actions.

### Examples of Executive Decisions

- Disable VPN access
- Switch operations to disaster recovery infrastructure
- Perform an Active Directory domain recovery
- Isolate business-critical systems
- Approve emergency operational changes

### Key Learning

Cyber crisis management is an ongoing process that continuously adapts as new information becomes available.

---

# Phase 5 — Subject Matter Experts (SMEs)

One of the most important discoveries during this project was the role of Subject Matter Experts.

Executives cannot make effective decisions without accurate technical information.

SMEs bridge the gap between technical operations and executive leadership.

## Responsibilities of SMEs

- Explain the technical scope of the incident
- Recommend possible response actions
- Estimate business impact
- Assess recovery options
- Provide risk assessments
- Answer executive questions

### Example Contributions

- Backup availability
- Disaster Recovery readiness
- Recovery timelines
- Infrastructure dependencies
- Operational impacts

### Key Learning

Executive decisions are only as effective as the quality of information provided by SMEs.

---

# Phase 6 — Crisis Communications

Managing communication is one of the CMT's highest priorities.

Poor communication can damage public trust even if the technical response is successful.

## Internal Communications

Prepared for:

- Employees
- IT Teams
- Help Desk
- Management

Holding statements help reduce confusion while investigations continue.

---

## External Communications

Prepared for:

- Customers
- Business partners
- Media
- Investors

The objective is to maintain transparency while avoiding speculation or unnecessary panic.

---

## Regulatory Notifications

Depending on the organization and applicable laws, regulators may need to be notified.

Examples include:

- Financial regulators
- Data protection authorities
- Privacy regulators

Timely reporting supports legal compliance and organizational accountability.

---

## Law Enforcement

Law enforcement agencies may also be engaged to assist with:

- Criminal investigations
- Evidence handling
- Digital forensic support
- Preservation of chain of custody

# Key Concepts Learned

- Cyber crises require executive leadership beyond traditional incident response.
- Incident severity determines when the Crisis Management Team should be activated.
- The Golden Hour is critical for establishing control of the crisis.
- Subject Matter Experts provide the technical insight needed for executive decision-making.
- Crisis management follows a continuous cycle of information gathering, triage, discussion, and action.
- Effective communication protects organizational reputation during crises.
- Regulatory and law enforcement obligations should be incorporated into crisis response planning.
- Thorough documentation supports accountability and future process improvements.
